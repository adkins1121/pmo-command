1. Executive Summary
This program replaces ABES Inc.'s QuickBooks Online accounting system - and several adjacent point-solution SaaS tools - with a self-hosted, open-source ERPNext platform that AMDG (Atlas Medical Data Group) owns and operates. ABES is an Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) therapy provider operating clinics in Geneva and Sycamore, Illinois, so the eventual system must handle both ordinary business finances and, in later phases, clinical patient data under HIPAA. The program is deliberately sequenced to deliver the financial foundation first, with no protected health information (PHI) anywhere in the early phases, and to introduce clinical data only once that foundation is proven and reconciled.
This is a multi-vendor program run as one coordinated effort. A transformation lead (Mike Adkins, AltusKC) sits at the center as AMDG's program owner - holding the integrated roadmap, and keeping the vendors aligned. Nexera Digital does the hands-on engineering build inside AMDG's project. Change Shepherd Group provides the advisory and change layer: systems discovery, the data and chart-of-accounts design that the migration is built against, organizational change leadership at the clinics, and reporting strategy. AMDG owns the platform and the decisions. The purpose of this charter is to define that scope clearly - what the program is, what each work stream delivers, and how the pieces fit together.
Strategically, AMDG's CEO frames Phase 1 as the "box killer": a proof that an AMDG-owned, AI-extensible ERPNext can replace a fragmented SaaS stack end-to-end. Success is measured not by "the data moved" but by "QuickBooks and its satellites are switched off and the automation runs in ERPNext."
2. Background &amp; Context
ABES currently runs its finances on QuickBooks Online (QBO) Plus, surrounded by a patchwork of disconnected systems - Rethink (clinical/practice management), EasyClaim (billing and claims), Timeco via the OneDigital PEO (time and payroll), and others - with no consolidated view such as gross profit by clinic location. This stack is fragmented and not extensible with the AI-driven automation AMDG wants to layer on. AMDG already runs its own books on a comparable self-hosted ERPNext stack, so the target patterns are proven rather than experimental.
This project exists because, rather than rebuild from scratch, AMDG combined the useful capabilities from seven prior ERPNext-related projects into one home, governed by a single architecture and quality standard. The table below shows what was harvested from where.

2.1 The reset (why Phase 1 looks the way it does)
Phase 1 was reframed at a 2026-05-12 project sync, recorded in decision record ADR-005. The original engagement assumed a migration-only Phase 1 executed against a separate codebase; that was reset so the build happens directly inside AMDG's project, under AMDG's architecture and quality bar. At the same time, AMDG's own live ERPNext bookkeeping had already de-risked the financial-automation work, and leadership made clear that a migration-only Phase 1 would produce no real change versus QuickBooks. The decision: Phase 1 expanded from "migrate QuickBooks data" to a financial-automation bundle (data migration + financial automation + document management), under strict simplifying constraints - one-way export-based migration, no patient data, and chart-of-accounts reconciliation as the non-negotiable backbone.
2.2 A multi-vendor program, coordinated by a transformation lead
Phase 1 is delivered by several parties with deliberately separated roles, coordinated as one program. The transformation lead (Mike Adkins, AltusKC) is the single operator who sees across all the vendors and work streams - he owns the integrated roadmap and the operating cadence, so AMDG does not have to coordinate the vendors directly. Nexera Digital is the build partner. Change Shepherd Group is the advisory and change-management partner, whose discovery and design work defines the target the build is aimed at. The work streams in Section 11 are organized by capability and note who leads each one; the transformation lead is the governance layer over all of them (Section 6.1), not a work stream itself.
Change Shepherd's discovery also widens the system landscape beyond QuickBooks and Rethink: ABES's stack additionally includes EasyClaim (billing/claims) and Timeco via the OneDigital PEO (payroll). CSG is further advising on a multi-entity architecture - clinic instances, a holding company, and a planned MSO instance - designed for consolidated reporting across current and future acquisitions. That forward-looking, multi-entity framing sits above this charter's current ABES-only scope and is the most likely source of future scope change; it warrants its own decision record before the chart-of-accounts design is finalized.
3. Program Vision &amp; Objectives
Vision:
An AMDG-owned, AI-extensible ERPNext platform that fully replaces ABES's QuickBooks-centered SaaS stack and, over later phases, absorbs clinical operations (patient/provider data and scheduling) under HIPAA-grade controls.
Strategic objectives:
O1 - Prove the "box killer" thesis - open-source ERPNext plus AI can replace constrained SaaS end-to-end for healthcare-adjacent finance and operations.
O2 - Eliminate QuickBooks Online and adjacent per-seat SaaS tools, consolidating onto one owned platform with consolidated cross-clinic reporting.
O3 - Deliver automation value (receivables, invoice OCR, bank feeds, document sync) in the same phase as migration, not after it.
O4 - Establish a repeatable, AI-safe way of working that lets human and AI contributors ship in two-week sprints without cross-cutting risk.
O5 - Lay a clean foundation so clinical migration and scheduling can be added later under HIPAA controls without rebuilding.

6. Governance &amp; Operating Model
6.1 Program governance &amp; transformation management
AMDG has engaged a transformation lead - Mike Adkins of AltusKC - as a fractional resource who sees across every vendor and work stream, so AMDG does not have to manage the engagement vendor-by-vendor. His remit is the transformation management office: owning the integrated roadmap that sequences work across teams and vendors by effort, risk, and dependency, holding vendors accountable for pace and value, driving HIPAA compliance enablement so later clinical phases are not blocked, and running the operating cadence and steering committee. He partners with Charles Stoops on the AMDG side. This is the governance layer over all of the work streams in Section 11; it is deliberately not itself a delivery work stream, because its output is coordination, sequencing, and decisions rather than a slice of the product.
6.2 How the system is built
The system is built feature by feature rather than in technical layers. Each business capability is a self-contained unit that includes everything it needs, along with its own tests. The practical payoff is that one capability can be built or changed without disturbing the others, a new contributor can focus on just the piece they own, and a mistake in one area is contained rather than rippling across the whole system. That containment also makes it safe to let AI assist: an AI error in one feature can't spread elsewhere. Shared building blocks are factored out only once several features genuinely need the same thing. (See ADR-001.)
6.3 How the work is done - test-first and locked
Every feature starts by writing down, in advance, exactly how it should behave and what would prove it works - and that proof is written as an automated test before the feature itself is built. Those tests are then locked: the team, including any AI assistant, may add new tests but may never quietly weaken or delete an existing one to make a problem appear to pass. An automated checkpoint enforces this. Work proceeds in small, disciplined increments, each one written, tested, cleaned up, and checked in on its own. (See ADR-002.)

10. Success Criteria
Phase 1 is complete when, and only when:
The QuickBooks migration criteria pass (extraction, transformation, loading, and reconciliation).
The financial-automation criteria pass (intake, classification, and accounting entries).
The document-management criteria pass (inbound and outbound sync).
The new system's totals match QuickBooks to the penny, and the detailed customer/vendor balances reconcile.
All 18 quality checks pass for every change, with no patient data introduced on any branch.
The automation demonstrably runs against the migrated data - migration alone does not count as done.
